Zombie Run is a browser-based interactive apocalypse survival game that combines real-time computer vision, motion tracking, and meme-driven “brain rot” gameplay into a physically engaging experience inspired by Subway Surfers. Built using React, TypeScript, TailwindCSS, and a lightweight backend architecture, the game uses the player’s webcam and real-time pose estimation to transform the user into the main character of a zombie apocalypse scenario where survival depends entirely on physical movement and reaction speed. 
After entering the website, players are presented with a stylized dystopian landing page featuring animated zombie visuals and glitch effects. Once the run is started, the system activates the webcam and performs a short calibration phase using MediaPipe Pose estimation to determine the player’s body position, running baseline, movement thresholds, and gesture boundaries. 
During gameplay, the player must physically run in place in front of their laptop to generate forward movement within the game, where running intensity directly controls the distance between the player and the zombies chasing them from behind. The interface displays the live webcam feed of the player with dynamically composited 2D zombie graphics and animated visual effects layered behind them, creating the illusion of being chased in real time. Throughout the game, random obstacle prompts appear on screen requiring the player to perform specific body motions such as jumping, dodging left, dodging right, or performing the viral “67” TikTok hand gesture while continuing to run. The game continuously evaluates player motion using lightweight landmark-based heuristics and pose tracking algorithms optimized for smooth browser performance and low latency. Successfully completing obstacle motions increases the player’s survival chances by pushing zombies farther away, while failed or incorrect actions immediately reduce the distance between the player and the pursuing zombies. A hidden dynamic stamina and distance system governs the overall survival mechanics, where zombies gradually move closer if the player slows down, stops running, or misses obstacle prompts, eventually triggering a game-over state once the zombies catch the player. The game includes a combo mechanic where successfully completing three obstacle challenges consecutively activates a temporary “Brain Rot Boost” that increases the distance from zombies and triggers exaggerated meme-inspired visual and audio effects. Difficulty scaling starts at an easy pace and automatically increases over time, introducing faster zombie acceleration, more frequent obstacles, tighter gesture timing windows, and shorter expected survival durations the longer the player survives. 
The system is designed with performance and accessibility as a priority by running all computer vision inference client-side in the browser using optimized low-resolution webcam processing and reduced pose-estimation frame rates to maintain smooth gameplay on standard laptops without requiring specialized hardware or server-side video processing.
